Scam self-check

Pick what you're worried about and answer a few plain questions. You'll get an honest read on the warning signs.

This is a transparent checklist based on common scam patterns — not AI, and not a verdict.

What to do next

  1. Stop all contact and payments. Don't send money, share codes, or grant remote access.
  2. Tell your bank or payment provider immediately — they may be able to stop or reverse a transfer.
  3. Change passwords on any account that may be exposed, and turn on two-factor authentication.
  4. Report it to the authorities below. Reporting helps others even if you're unsure.
  5. Keep the evidence — messages, screenshots, receipts, phone numbers, and links.

No legitimate service — including this one — will ever ask you to pay to recover your money. Anyone who does is scamming you.

Where to report

Start with your local police and your bank. Then report to the relevant body for your country:

  • 🌍 International consumer fraud: econsumer.gov
  • 💻 Online / internet crime: ic3.gov
  • 🇺🇸 United States: reportfraud.ftc.gov
  • 🇬🇧 United Kingdom: Action Fraud
  • 🇦🇺 Australia: Scamwatch
  • 🇨🇦 Canada: Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre

Not listed? Search your country's name plus “report fraud” or ask a volunteer and we'll point you to the right place.
