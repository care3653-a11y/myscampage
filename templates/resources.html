Resources & prevention

Know the patterns, protect yourself, and find the official places to report — wherever you are.

Common scam alerts

  • Crypto & investment — fake platforms promising high or guaranteed returns.
  • Romance — trust built over time, then an urgent request for money.
  • Impersonation — fake calls or messages from “your bank,” police, or a company.
  • Recovery scams — someone promising to get your lost money back, for a fee. Always fake.

How to protect yourself

  • ✅ Slow down — pressure and urgency are the scammer's main tools.
  • ✅ Verify independently using contact details you look up yourself.
  • ✅ Never share passwords or one-time codes with anyone.
  • ✅ Be wary of gift-card, crypto, or wire-transfer payment demands.
  • ✅ Turn on two-factor authentication everywhere you can.
  • ✅ Run our self-check when unsure.

Where to report a scam

First, always: contact your local police and your bank or payment provider — speed matters for stopping transfers. Then report to the relevant body:

🌍 International

econsumer.gov — cross-border consumer fraud, run by a coalition of governments.

💻 Online crime

ic3.gov — internet crime complaints, accepts international reports.

🇺🇸 United States

reportfraud.ftc.gov

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

Action Fraud

🇦🇺 Australia

Scamwatch

🇨🇦 Canada

Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre

In another country? Search your country's name plus “report fraud,” or and we'll help you find the right authority.
